Best States for Remote Insurance Jobs in the U.S.

Home / Blog / Blog Details

The rise of remote work has transformed the job market, and the insurance industry is no exception. With companies embracing flexible work arrangements, professionals now have the freedom to choose where they live while advancing their careers. But not all states are created equal when it comes to remote insurance jobs. Factors like cost of living, tax benefits, internet infrastructure, and industry demand play a crucial role in determining the best locations.

Here’s a deep dive into the top states for remote insurance jobs in 2024, along with key insights to help you make an informed decision.

Why Remote Insurance Jobs Are Booming

The insurance sector has seen a significant shift toward remote work, driven by technological advancements and changing workplace expectations. Here’s why:

Digital Transformation in Insurance

Insurtech innovations, AI-driven underwriting, and cloud-based platforms have made it easier than ever for insurance professionals to work from anywhere. Companies are investing in digital tools that streamline claims processing, customer service, and policy management.

Talent Demand Outweighs Location Constraints

With a growing need for actuaries, underwriters, and claims adjusters, employers are prioritizing skills over physical presence. This has opened doors for remote workers in states with lower competition but high quality of life.

Work-Life Balance Priorities

Post-pandemic, employees value flexibility more than ever. Remote insurance jobs allow professionals to avoid long commutes, reduce stress, and spend more time with family—all while maintaining career growth.

Top States for Remote Insurance Jobs

1. Texas

Texas is a powerhouse for insurance professionals, thanks to its business-friendly environment and no state income tax. Major insurers like USAA and State Farm have a strong presence here, creating ample remote job opportunities.

Key Benefits:

  • Affordable Housing: Cities like Austin and Dallas offer reasonable living costs compared to coastal hubs.
  • Strong Job Market: High demand for underwriters and claims specialists.
  • Reliable Internet: Texas ranks among the top states for broadband access.

2. Florida

With no state income tax and a booming insurance market (especially in property and casualty due to climate risks), Florida is a top pick for remote workers.

Key Benefits:

  • Growing Insurtech Scene: Miami is emerging as a hub for digital insurance startups.
  • Low Cost of Living: Compared to New York or California, Florida offers more affordability.
  • Networking Opportunities: Annual insurance conferences and events attract industry leaders.

3. North Carolina

North Carolina combines Southern charm with a thriving financial services sector. Charlotte, known as the second-largest banking hub in the U.S., also hosts major insurance firms.

Key Benefits:

  • Tech-Savvy Workforce: Strong IT infrastructure supports remote work seamlessly.
  • Moderate Taxes: Lower tax burdens than many Northeastern states.
  • Quality of Life: Scenic landscapes and vibrant cities like Raleigh make it an attractive place to live.

4. Colorado

For those who love the outdoors without sacrificing career growth, Colorado is ideal. Denver’s insurance market is expanding, and remote roles are plentiful.

Key Benefits:

  • Healthy Work Culture: Companies emphasize employee well-being and flexibility.
  • High-Speed Internet: Ranked among the best states for connectivity.
  • Progressive Policies: Strong support for remote work legislation.

5. Georgia

Atlanta is a hotspot for insurance corporations, including Aflac and Delta Life. The state’s low cost of living makes it a smart choice for remote professionals.

Key Benefits:

  • Thriving Industry Hub: Home to multiple Fortune 500 insurance companies.
  • Affordable Living: Housing costs are significantly lower than in coastal cities.
  • Diverse Job Openings: Roles range from sales to data analytics.

Emerging Trends in Remote Insurance Work

The Rise of Hybrid Roles

While fully remote jobs are abundant, some companies prefer hybrid models—especially for roles requiring occasional in-person collaboration. States with clustered insurance offices (like Illinois or Pennsylvania) may offer more hybrid opportunities.

Specialized Niches in High Demand

  • Cyber Insurance: With increasing cyber threats, experts in this niche are highly sought after.
  • Climate Risk Underwriting: States prone to natural disasters (e.g., Florida, California) need specialists to assess climate-related risks.

The Impact of AI

Automation is reshaping insurance jobs, but rather than replacing workers, it’s augmenting roles. Remote professionals skilled in AI tools (e.g., predictive modeling software) will have a competitive edge.

How to Land a Remote Insurance Job

Build a Strong Digital Presence

  • Optimize your LinkedIn profile with relevant keywords (e.g., "remote underwriter," "claims analyst").
  • Showcase certifications like CPCU or ARM to stand out.

Target the Right Companies

Research insurers known for remote-friendly policies, such as Progressive, Liberty Mutual, or Nationwide.

Leverage Job Platforms

Websites like FlexJobs, We Work Remotely, and Indeed filter remote insurance roles by state.

Negotiate Flexibility

Even if a job posting doesn’t explicitly state "remote," many employers are open to negotiation—especially for high-demand roles.

Final Thoughts

The best state for your remote insurance career depends on your priorities: affordability, job availability, or lifestyle perks. Whether you’re drawn to Texas’ tax benefits, Florida’s insurtech growth, or Colorado’s work-life balance, the opportunities are vast. As remote work continues to evolve, staying adaptable and tech-savvy will ensure long-term success in this dynamic industry.
