How to File a Claim with Progressive for a Collision Repair

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Navigating the aftermath of a car accident can be overwhelming, especially when dealing with repairs and insurance claims. With rising fuel costs, supply chain disruptions, and the increasing complexity of modern vehicles, understanding how to efficiently file a collision repair claim with Progressive Insurance is more important than ever. This guide will walk you through the process step by step, ensuring you get your vehicle back on the road as quickly as possible.

Understanding Progressive’s Collision Coverage

Before filing a claim, it’s essential to know what your policy covers. Progressive offers several types of auto insurance, but collision coverage is specifically designed to pay for repairs to your vehicle after an accident, regardless of who is at fault.

What Does Collision Coverage Include?

  • Repairs to your car after a collision with another vehicle or object.
  • Damage from single-car accidents (e.g., hitting a guardrail).
  • Coverage for hit-and-run incidents (varies by state).

What Isn’t Covered?

  • Routine maintenance or wear and tear.
  • Damage from non-collision events (e.g., theft or natural disasters—these fall under comprehensive coverage).

Step-by-Step Guide to Filing a Collision Claim

1. Ensure Safety First

After an accident, check for injuries and call 911 if necessary. Move to a safe location if possible and exchange information with the other driver(s).

2. Document the Scene

  • Take photos of the damage, license plates, and the accident scene.
  • Note the time, location, and weather conditions.
  • Collect witness statements if available.

3. Report the Accident to Progressive

You can file a claim in several ways:
- Online: Log in to your Progressive account and submit a claim.
- Mobile App: Use the Progressive app for quick filing.
- Phone: Call Progressive’s claims department at 1-800-PROGRESSIVE (1-800-776-4737).

4. Provide Necessary Information

When filing, have the following details ready:
- Your policy number.
- The other driver’s insurance information (if applicable).
- Police report number (if filed).
- Photos and documentation from the scene.

5. Schedule an Inspection

Progressive may require an inspection to assess the damage. You can:
- Visit a Progressive-approved repair shop.
- Use a virtual inspection tool (available in some states).

6. Choose a Repair Shop

You have the right to select your own repair shop, but Progressive’s network shops often offer guarantees on workmanship.

7. Review the Estimate

Progressive will provide a repair estimate. If you disagree, you can negotiate or seek a second opinion from another shop.

8. Pay Your Deductible

Once repairs are approved, you’ll need to pay your deductible before Progressive covers the remaining costs.

9. Track Your Claim

Use Progressive’s online portal or app to monitor the status of your claim and repairs.

Navigating Modern Challenges in Collision Repairs

Supply Chain Delays

With global supply chain issues, some auto parts may take longer to arrive. Progressive may authorize aftermarket or used parts to expedite repairs.

Rising Repair Costs

Due to inflation and advanced vehicle technology, repair costs have increased. Ensure your coverage limits are adequate.

Electric and Hybrid Vehicles

Repairing EVs and hybrids requires specialized technicians. Verify that your chosen shop is certified for these vehicles.

Tips for a Smooth Claims Process

  • Be proactive: File your claim as soon as possible.
  • Keep records: Save all receipts and communication with Progressive.
  • Understand your rights: You’re not obligated to use Progressive’s recommended shops.

By following these steps, you can streamline your collision repair claim with Progressive and minimize stress during an already challenging time.
