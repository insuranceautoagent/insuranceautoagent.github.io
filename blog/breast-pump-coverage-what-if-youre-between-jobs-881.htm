Breast Pump Coverage: What If You’re Between Jobs?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Navigating healthcare benefits can be challenging, especially when you’re between jobs. For new or expecting mothers, access to a breast pump is often a critical need, but what happens when you’re in a transition period with no employer-sponsored insurance? This article explores your options, the current landscape of breast pump coverage, and how to secure this essential tool even during unemployment.

Understanding Breast Pump Coverage Under the ACA

The Affordable Care Act (ACA) mandates that most private health insurance plans cover the cost of a breast pump as part of preventive care for pregnant and nursing mothers. However, this coverage typically applies only if you’re enrolled in an employer-sponsored plan or a marketplace plan.

Types of Breast Pumps Covered

  • Manual Pumps: Usually fully covered but require more effort.
  • Electric Pumps: Often partially or fully covered, depending on the plan.
  • Hospital-Grade Pumps: Typically require a doctor’s prescription and may only be covered for specific medical needs.

What Happens When You Lose Job-Based Insurance?

Losing your job doesn’t necessarily mean losing access to a breast pump, but it does require some strategic planning. Here’s what you need to know:

COBRA Continuation Coverage

If you’ve recently left a job, you may be eligible for COBRA, which allows you to extend your employer-sponsored health plan for a limited time (usually 18 months). Under COBRA:
- Your breast pump benefits remain intact.
- You’ll pay the full premium (which can be expensive).
- You must enroll within 60 days of losing coverage.

Medicaid and State Programs

If you’re unemployed and meet income requirements, Medicaid may cover a breast pump. Coverage varies by state, but many Medicaid programs include this benefit.

Marketplace Plans (Healthcare.gov)

If you’re between jobs, you may qualify for a Special Enrollment Period to sign up for an ACA marketplace plan. These plans must comply with ACA guidelines, meaning breast pump coverage is included.

Alternative Options If You Don’t Have Insurance

If you’re uninsured and don’t qualify for Medicaid or marketplace plans, there are still ways to get a breast pump:

Nonprofit Organizations

Several charities and nonprofits provide free or low-cost breast pumps to mothers in need. Examples include:
- The Breastfeeding Shop (offers discounted pumps).
- Helping Hands for Single Moms (supports low-income mothers).

Manufacturer Discounts

Some breast pump companies offer financial assistance programs or payment plans for those without insurance.

Renting a Hospital-Grade Pump

If you only need a pump short-term, renting from a hospital or medical supply store can be a cost-effective solution.

The Bigger Picture: Why Breast Pump Access Matters

Breastfeeding has significant health benefits for both babies and mothers, but barriers like cost and insurance gaps can make it difficult. Advocacy groups are pushing for expanded coverage, especially for unemployed and low-income mothers.

The Role of Employers and Policymakers

  • More companies should offer extended benefits during layoffs.
  • States could expand Medicaid coverage for breastfeeding supplies.
  • Federal policies could strengthen protections for unemployed mothers.

Final Tips for Securing a Breast Pump Between Jobs

  1. Check Your Eligibility – Explore COBRA, Medicaid, or marketplace plans.
  2. Act Quickly – Enrollment windows for COBRA and ACA plans are time-sensitive.
  3. Reach Out for Help – Nonprofits and lactation consultants can guide you.

Being between jobs shouldn’t mean sacrificing your ability to provide for your baby. With the right information and resources, you can still access the tools you need for a healthy breastfeeding journey.
