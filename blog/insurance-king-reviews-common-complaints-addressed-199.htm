Insurance King Reviews: Common Complaints Addressed

Home / Blog / Blog Details

When it comes to insurance providers, few names spark as much debate as Insurance King. Known for its aggressive marketing and budget-friendly policies, the company has built a reputation—both good and bad. But what do customers really think? In this deep dive, we’ll address the most common complaints about Insurance King, separating fact from fiction and offering solutions for policyholders.

1. Claims Processing Delays: Why It Happens and How to Fix It

The Complaint

One of the top grievances in Insurance King reviews revolves around slow claims processing. Customers report waiting weeks (or even months) for payouts, leaving them frustrated during critical moments.

The Reality

Insurance King operates as a non-standard auto insurer, meaning they often serve high-risk drivers. This can lead to:
- Higher scrutiny – More fraud attempts mean stricter verification.
- Understaffing – High claim volumes overwhelm adjusters.
- Third-party dependencies – Repairs shops or medical providers delay documentation.

How to Speed Up Your Claim

  • Submit all documents immediately (photos, police reports, medical bills).
  • Follow up every 48 hours with your adjuster.
  • Escalate to a supervisor if delays exceed 10 business days.

2. Premium Hikes: Why Your Rates Suddenly Jumped

The Complaint

Policyholders often report unexpected premium increases, sometimes mid-term, with no clear explanation.

The Triggers

Insurance King uses dynamic pricing, meaning rates adjust based on:
- Driving record changes (new tickets, accidents).
- Credit score fluctuations (yes, they check).
- Regional risk spikes (e.g., more thefts in your ZIP code).

Fighting Back

  • Ask for a re-evaluation – Errors happen.
  • Increase your deductible – Lower monthly costs (but higher out-of-pocket later).
  • Bundle policies – Discounts for multi-line customers.

3. Customer Service Woes: Long Holds, Unhelpful Agents

The Complaint

“I waited on hold for 90 minutes just to get transferred to the wrong department!” Sound familiar?

Why It Happens

  • High turnover – Low wages lead to inexperienced staff.
  • Outdated systems – Clunky software slows resolutions.
  • Volume spikes – Post-accident or disaster call surges.

Pro Tips for Better Service

  • Use online portals for simple requests (policy changes, ID cards).
  • Call early mornings or late evenings (shorter wait times).
  • Request a callback option instead of holding.

4. Denied Claims: Top Reasons and Appeals Tactics

The Complaint

“Insurance King denied my claim for no reason!”

Common Denial Causes

  • Lapsed payments – Even a 1-day delay can void coverage.
  • Policy exclusions – Read the fine print (e.g., rideshare accidents).
  • Late reporting – Most states require immediate accident notification.

Winning an Appeal

  • Gather evidence – Witness statements, dashcam footage.
  • Cite state laws – Some denials violate regulatory mandates.
  • Hire a public adjuster – Professionals negotiate for you.

5. Transparency Issues: Hidden Fees and Fine Print Traps

The Complaint

“My policy had fees I never agreed to!”

Where Fees Hide

  • Installment payments – Monthly fees vs. paying annually.
  • Cancellation penalties – Up to 10% of remaining premium.
  • Mysterious surcharges – “Policy recovery fees” after missed payments.

How to Avoid Surprises

  • Demand a full breakdown before signing.
  • Opt for paperless billing – Alerts for new charges.
  • File a complaint with your state’s DOI if fees seem fraudulent.

6. The “Cheap Premiums, High Out-of-Pocket” Trap

The Complaint

“My monthly rate was low, but I paid thousands after my accident!”

The Trade-Off

Insurance King attracts budget-conscious drivers with low premiums, but:
- High deductibles – Often $1,000+ for collisions.
- Limited coverage – Basic liability won’t cover your own repairs.
- Depreciation payouts – Older cars get “actual cash value,” not replacement cost.

Smart Adjustments

  • Add rental coverage – Avoid out-of-pocket car rental fees.
  • Consider gap insurance – If you owe more than your car’s value.
  • Review annually – Upgrade as your financial situation improves.

Final Thoughts

While Insurance King fills a niche for affordable coverage, it’s not without flaws. By understanding these common complaints—and how to navigate them—you can make informed decisions. Whether it’s fighting a denied claim or avoiding fee traps, knowledge is your best defense.
