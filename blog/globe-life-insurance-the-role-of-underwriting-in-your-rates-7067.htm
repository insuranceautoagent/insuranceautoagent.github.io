Globe Life Insurance: The Role of Underwriting in Your Rates

Home / Blog / Blog Details

When it comes to life insurance, one of the most critical yet misunderstood aspects is underwriting. Whether you're applying for a policy with Globe Life Insurance or another provider, the underwriting process plays a pivotal role in determining your premiums. But what exactly is underwriting, and how does it influence the rates you pay? More importantly, how do modern challenges—like climate change, economic instability, and evolving health risks—impact underwriting today?

What Is Underwriting in Life Insurance?

Underwriting is the process insurers use to evaluate the risk of insuring you. It involves assessing factors like your age, health, lifestyle, occupation, and even your family medical history. Based on this evaluation, the insurer decides whether to approve your application and at what premium rate.

The Traditional Underwriting Process

Historically, underwriting relied heavily on:
- Medical exams (blood tests, blood pressure checks, etc.)
- Questionnaires about your health and habits
- Public records (e.g., driving history, criminal background)
- Financial assessments (income, debts, etc.)

However, with advancements in AI, big data, and predictive analytics, underwriting has evolved.

How Underwriting Affects Your Globe Life Insurance Rates

Your final premium isn’t arbitrary—it’s calculated based on risk. Here’s how different factors play into your rates:

1. Health Status

If you have pre-existing conditions like diabetes, heart disease, or high cholesterol, you may face higher premiums. Some insurers, including Globe Life, offer no-medical-exam policies, but these often come with higher base rates to offset the unknown risk.

2. Age and Gender

Younger applicants typically get lower rates because they’re statistically less likely to file a claim soon. Women often pay slightly less than men due to longer life expectancy.

3. Lifestyle Choices

  • Smokers pay significantly more—sometimes 2-3x higher premiums.
  • High-risk hobbies (e.g., skydiving, deep-sea diving) can increase costs.
  • Alcohol consumption beyond moderate levels may also raise rates.

4. Occupation and Environment

Jobs involving physical danger (e.g., firefighters, miners) may lead to higher premiums. Additionally, living in areas prone to natural disasters or pollution could subtly influence rates due to associated health risks.

Modern Challenges Impacting Underwriting

The insurance industry isn’t immune to global trends. Here’s how today’s biggest issues are reshaping underwriting:

Climate Change and Mortality Risk

Rising temperatures, extreme weather, and increased pollution are linked to higher rates of respiratory diseases, heat strokes, and cardiovascular issues. Insurers are now factoring in geographic risk assessments when setting premiums.

The Pandemic’s Long-Term Effects

COVID-19 has left many with long-term health complications, forcing insurers to adjust underwriting models. Some now consider:
- Post-COVID conditions (e.g., lung damage, chronic fatigue)
- Vaccination status (though most U.S. insurers no longer penalize the unvaccinated)

Economic Inflation and Policy Costs

As healthcare costs rise, so do insurance payouts. This economic pressure means insurers must recalibrate risk models, sometimes leading to higher premiums across the board.

How Globe Life Insurance Adapts to These Changes

Globe Life, like many insurers, has integrated data-driven underwriting tools to stay competitive. Some key adaptations include:

1. Digital Underwriting

Many applicants can now skip medical exams by allowing insurers to analyze electronic health records (EHRs) and wearable device data (e.g., Fitbit, Apple Watch).

2. Behavioral Risk Scoring

Some insurers use AI algorithms to assess risk based on non-traditional data, such as:
- Shopping habits (e.g., organic food purchases = lower risk)
- Social media activity (though controversial)

3. Customized Policies

Rather than a one-size-fits-all approach, Globe Life and others now offer more flexible underwriting tiers, allowing applicants with minor health issues to still get affordable coverage.

Tips to Secure the Best Rates

If you’re applying for life insurance, here’s how to navigate underwriting in your favor:

1. Apply Sooner Rather Than Later

Every year you age, premiums tend to increase. Locking in a rate at 35 is cheaper than at 45.

2. Improve Your Health Metrics

  • Quit smoking (most insurers require at least 1 year smoke-free for better rates).
  • Lower BMI through diet and exercise.
  • Manage chronic conditions with regular doctor visits.

3. Compare Multiple Quotes

Underwriting standards vary by company. One insurer might penalize a mild anxiety diagnosis, while another may not.

4. Consider No-Exam Policies (If Healthy)

If you’re in excellent health, a no-medical-exam policy might be cost-effective. But if you have underlying conditions, a traditional underwritten policy could actually be cheaper.

The Future of Underwriting

With AI, genetic testing, and real-time health monitoring becoming mainstream, underwriting will only get more precise—and possibly more intrusive. Some emerging trends include:

  • DNA-based risk assessment (though currently restricted by laws like GINA in the U.S.).
  • Continuous underwriting, where your premiums adjust based on real-time health data.
  • Climate-adjusted pricing, where insurers factor in environmental risks more heavily.

While these changes promise fairer pricing for low-risk individuals, they also raise privacy concerns. The balance between personalized rates and data security will be a key debate in the coming years.

For now, understanding how underwriting works—and how Globe Life Insurance approaches it—can help you make smarter decisions when shopping for coverage. Whether you’re a young professional or someone managing health challenges, the right policy is out there—you just need to know how to find it.
