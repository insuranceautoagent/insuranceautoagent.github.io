Jackson National Life Insurance: A Guide to Their Non-Qualified Annuities

Home / Blog / Blog Details

In today’s volatile economic landscape, securing a stable financial future has never been more critical. With inflation, market unpredictability, and shifting retirement policies, many investors are turning to annuities as a safeguard. Among the top providers, Jackson National Life Insurance stands out, particularly for its non-qualified annuities. But what makes these products unique, and how can they fit into your financial strategy?

Understanding Non-Qualified Annuities

What Are Non-Qualified Annuities?

Unlike qualified annuities, which are funded with pre-tax dollars (e.g., through an IRA or 401(k)), non-qualified annuities are purchased with after-tax money. This distinction affects taxation, withdrawal rules, and flexibility.

Why Choose a Non-Qualified Annuity?

  • Tax-Deferred Growth: Earnings accumulate tax-free until withdrawal.
  • No Contribution Limits: Unlike IRAs, there’s no annual cap on contributions.
  • Flexible Payout Options: Choose between lump sums, systematic withdrawals, or lifetime income streams.

Jackson National’s Competitive Edge

Strong Financial Ratings

Jackson National boasts high ratings from A.M. Best (A+) and Standard & Poor’s (AA-), signaling financial stability—a must in today’s uncertain markets.

Innovative Product Offerings

Jackson’s Perspective II and Elite Access annuities provide:
- Index-linked growth tied to market performance (without direct market risk).
- Optional riders for enhanced death benefits or income guarantees.

Addressing Modern Retirement Challenges

With rising life expectancies and pension cuts, Jackson’s annuities help mitigate:
- Longevity risk (outliving savings).
- Sequence-of-returns risk (market downturns early in retirement).

Key Considerations Before Investing

Fees and Surrender Charges

Non-qualified annuities often include:
- Mortality and expense fees (~1.25% annually).
- Surrender periods (e.g., 7–10 years with penalties for early withdrawals).

Tax Implications

  • Earnings are taxed as ordinary income upon withdrawal.
  • No step-up in basis at death (unlike stocks or real estate).

Liquidity Needs

Annuities are long-term vehicles. Assess whether you can lock away funds without hardship.

How Jackson’s Products Align with Global Trends

Inflation Hedging

With the U.S. inflation rate fluctuating, Jackson’s indexed annuities offer growth potential linked to benchmarks like the S&P 500—providing a hedge without market exposure.

ESG Investing

While not explicitly ESG-focused, Jackson’s parent company, Prudential plc, emphasizes sustainable investing—a growing priority for retirees.

Geopolitical Uncertainty

Amid global instability, annuities provide a fixed-income buffer against volatile equities or crypto markets.

Real-World Applications

Case Study: A Mid-Career Professional

A 45-year-old invests $200,000 in Jackson’s Perspective II. By retirement, tax-deferred growth could supplement Social Security, especially if traditional pensions are unavailable.

Retirement Planning for Gig Workers

For freelancers without employer-sponsored plans, non-qualified annuities offer a structured savings path.

Final Thoughts

Jackson National’s non-qualified annuities are a compelling tool for modern retirement planning—blending security, growth potential, and adaptability. However, consult a financial advisor to ensure alignment with your goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation.
