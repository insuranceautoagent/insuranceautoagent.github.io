GEICO Flood Insurance: Is It Included or Separate?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

When it comes to protecting your home, understanding what your insurance policy covers is crucial. Flooding is one of the most common and devastating natural disasters, yet many homeowners mistakenly assume their standard homeowners insurance includes flood coverage. If you're a GEICO policyholder or considering their services, you might be wondering: Does GEICO flood insurance come bundled with homeowners insurance, or is it a separate policy?

Understanding Flood Insurance Basics

Before diving into GEICO’s offerings, it’s important to clarify how flood insurance works in the U.S.

Why Standard Homeowners Insurance Doesn’t Cover Floods

Most homeowners insurance policies, including those offered by GEICO, do not cover flood damage. Floods are considered a separate peril due to their catastrophic nature. Insurance companies typically exclude them from standard policies because of the high risk and potential for widespread damage.

The Role of the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP)

In the U.S., flood insurance is primarily provided through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP), a federal program managed by FEMA. Some private insurers, including GEICO, act as intermediaries, selling and servicing NFIP policies on behalf of the government.

GEICO’s Approach to Flood Insurance

GEICO does not underwrite its own flood insurance policies. Instead, it partners with the NFIP to offer flood coverage to homeowners and renters.

How GEICO Sells Flood Insurance

  1. NFIP Policies Through GEICO

    • GEICO acts as an agent for the NFIP, meaning they can help you purchase a government-backed flood insurance policy.
    • Coverage limits follow NFIP guidelines (up to $250,000 for dwelling and $100,000 for personal property).

  2. Private Flood Insurance Options

    • In some cases, GEICO may also connect customers with private flood insurance providers if they need higher coverage limits than what the NFIP offers.

Is Flood Insurance Included in GEICO Homeowners Policies?

No. GEICO’s standard homeowners insurance does not include flood coverage. You must purchase a separate flood insurance policy, either through the NFIP (via GEICO) or a private insurer.

Why Flood Insurance Is More Important Than Ever

With climate change intensifying weather patterns, flooding has become a growing concern—even in areas not traditionally considered high-risk.

Rising Flood Risks in Non-Coastal Areas

  • Urban flooding due to poor drainage and overdevelopment.
  • Flash floods from sudden heavy rainfall, which are becoming more frequent.
  • Riverine flooding as snowmelt and storms increase.

Financial Consequences of Being Uninsured

Without flood insurance, homeowners face:
- Out-of-pocket repair costs, which can easily exceed tens of thousands of dollars.
- Limited federal disaster aid, which often comes in the form of loans that must be repaid.

How to Get Flood Insurance Through GEICO

If you’re considering flood insurance, here’s how GEICO can help.

Step 1: Assess Your Flood Risk

  • Check FEMA’s Flood Map Service Center to see if your home is in a high-risk zone.
  • Even if you’re in a low or moderate-risk area, flood insurance may still be a wise investment.

Step 2: Request a Quote

  • Contact GEICO or visit their website to get a flood insurance quote.
  • Premiums vary based on location, elevation, and coverage amount.

Step 3: Understand Waiting Periods

  • NFIP policies have a 30-day waiting period before coverage begins (with few exceptions).
  • Don’t wait until a storm is forecasted—get coverage early.

Alternatives to NFIP Flood Insurance

If the NFIP’s coverage limits aren’t enough, GEICO may help you explore private flood insurance options, which can offer:
- Higher coverage limits (some up to $1 million or more).
- Additional living expenses if you’re displaced by a flood.
- Shorter waiting periods compared to the NFIP.

Final Thoughts

Flooding can happen anywhere, and standard homeowners insurance won’t protect you. GEICO makes it easy to add flood coverage through the NFIP, but it’s a separate policy—not included in your standard plan. Given the increasing frequency of floods, securing this coverage could save you from financial disaster.

If you’re unsure whether you need flood insurance, remember: It’s better to have it and not need it than to need it and not have it.
