How to Get Car Insurance After a DUI

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Getting car insurance after a DUI (Driving Under the Influence) conviction can feel like an uphill battle. With stricter regulations, higher premiums, and fewer options, many drivers struggle to find affordable coverage. However, a DUI doesn’t mean you’re permanently uninsurable. By understanding the process, exploring your options, and taking proactive steps, you can secure the coverage you need while rebuilding your driving record.

Understanding the Impact of a DUI on Car Insurance

A DUI is one of the most serious violations in the eyes of insurance companies. It signals high-risk behavior, leading to significant consequences:

Increased Premiums

After a DUI, insurers see you as a greater liability. Your rates can skyrocket—sometimes doubling or even tripling. According to recent studies, drivers with a DUI pay an average of 80-100% more for car insurance.

Limited Insurance Options

Many standard insurers may refuse coverage altogether, forcing you into the non-standard or high-risk insurance market. Companies specializing in high-risk drivers often charge much higher rates.

SR-22 or FR-44 Requirements

Most states require an SR-22 (or FR-44 in some cases) after a DUI. This is a certificate proving you carry the minimum required liability insurance. Without it, your license may remain suspended.

Steps to Get Car Insurance After a DUI

1. Compare Multiple Insurance Providers

Not all insurers treat DUIs the same. Some specialize in high-risk drivers and may offer better rates. Get quotes from:
- National insurers (e.g., GEICO, Progressive, State Farm)
- Regional or local insurers (sometimes more flexible)
- High-risk insurers (e.g., The General, Dairyland)

2. Consider Non-Owner Car Insurance (If You Don’t Own a Car)

If your license was suspended and you don’t own a vehicle but still need to drive occasionally, non-owner car insurance can be a temporary solution. It provides liability coverage when driving a borrowed or rented car.

3. Take a Defensive Driving Course

Completing an approved defensive driving course can sometimes lower your premiums. It shows insurers you’re committed to safer driving habits.

4. Opt for a Higher Deductible

Choosing a higher deductible (the amount you pay out-of-pocket before insurance kicks in) can reduce your monthly premiums. Just make sure you can afford the deductible if an accident occurs.

5. Improve Your Credit Score (If Applicable)

In many states, insurers use credit-based insurance scores to determine rates. Improving your credit can help lower premiums over time.

6. Avoid Further Violations

One DUI is bad enough—multiple offenses make insurance nearly impossible to obtain at reasonable rates. Drive safely, avoid tickets, and let time improve your record.

Long-Term Strategies to Lower Your Rates

Wait It Out

A DUI typically stays on your record for 3-10 years, depending on the state. Over time, its impact on your insurance rates will lessen.

Shop Around Annually

Insurance markets change, and your risk profile improves with time. Re-evaluate your policy every year to see if better rates are available.

Bundle Insurance Policies

Some insurers offer discounts if you bundle auto + home/renters insurance. Even with a DUI, bundling can save money.

Drive Less (Usage-Based Insurance)

If you don’t drive much, usage-based insurance (e.g., Progressive’s Snapshot) could lower costs. These programs track mileage and driving habits.

The Role of Technology in Post-DUI Insurance

With the rise of telematics and AI-driven underwriting, some insurers now offer more personalized rates. Usage-based programs reward safe driving, which can help offset high premiums after a DUI.

The Future of DUI and Insurance

As self-driving cars and ride-sharing become more common, DUIs may have different implications. However, for now, traditional insurance rules still apply.

Rebuilding trust with insurers takes time, but with persistence and smart choices, you can regain affordable coverage. Stay informed, drive responsibly, and explore all available options to get back on the road legally and financially protected.
